The present study conducts a comprehensive meta-analysis of randomized control trials that have implemented positive psychology in digital interventions. By aggregating data from a wide range of studies, we aim to assess the effectiveness and potential benefits of these interventions in promoting mental wellbeing and fostering resilience. Our analysis demonstrates a significant positive effect of digital positive psychology interventions, highlighting their importance in an increasingly digital age. The study also discusses potential mechanisms behind these effects, helping to elucidate how best to design and implement future digital interventions. Ultimately, this meta-analysis contributes to the growing body of evidence supporting the use of digital platforms for psychological interventions, with implications for both individual and societal mental health.
