title: "Lateralization of activation in the Superior Temporal Gyrus for Speech Processing in Sleeping Infants Is Predictive of Their Language Skills in Kindergarten: an fMRI Study"
collection: publications
permalink: /publication/project-3.3
excerpt: 'The study investigates the relationship between left-lateralized brain activation in the superior temporal gyrus (STG) during infancy and subsequent language skills in preschool/kindergarten. Using fMRI on 24 infants during natural sleep while listening to forward- versus backward-speech, researchers found that left-lateralization in the STG for forward speech at infancy was associated with later language skills, supporting the hypothesis of hemisphere asymmetries. However, no concurrent correlations were observed, and more studies are needed to replicate these findings due to the small sample size.'
citation: "Wang, J., Turesky, T., Loh, M., Barber, J., Hue, V., Escalante, E., Medina, A., Gaab, N.
