permalink: /publication/project-5
excerpt: 'This study examines the effects of adverse childhood experiences (ACEs) on children with neurodevelopmental disorders such as ASD and ADHD, focusing on how traits like curiosity, emotional control, and persistence might lessen these negative impacts. Utilizing data from the National Survey of Children's Health, logistic regression models revealed a significant correlation between ACEs and increased mental health issues, but also demonstrated that flourishing behaviors substantially mitigate these risks. The findings underscore the importance of incorporating flourishing-based approaches in interventions aimed at enhancing resilience and mental health outcomes for neurodivergent children.'
